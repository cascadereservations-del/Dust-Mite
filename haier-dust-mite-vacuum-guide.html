Maintenance & Care

Keep your vacuum performing optimally
1

üóëÔ∏è Empty Dust Container

After each use, detach and empty dual dust containers. Tap gently to remove all debris. Do not allow to become completely full.

2

üåä Clean Filter System

Remove HEPA filter every 2-4 weeks. Rinse with water and allow to dry COMPLETELY (24+ hours) before reinstalling. Never reinstall wet filter - will damage motor.

3

üí° Clean UV-C Lamp

Wipe UV lamp window with soft cloth and alcohol every month. Avoid touching with fingers - oils reduce sterilization effectiveness.

üßΩ Exterior Cleaning

Wipe exterior with soft, slightly damp cloth. Never immerse in water. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners.

5

üîÑ Replace Filter

Replace HEPA filter every 12-18 months depending on usage frequency. Use only genuine Haier replacement filters.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Maintenance Rules
  • NEVER wash filter with vacuum cleaner - hand rinse only

  • NEVER operate with wet filter or dust container
  • Always unplug before any maintenance
  • Do not disassemble device beyond dust container/filter
üí° Storage Tips

Store vacuum in charging base when not in use. If storing long-term, ensure completely clean and dry. Turn on for a few minutes monthly to maintain motor performance. Store in cool, ventilated area away from direct sunlight.

Understanding Dust Mites

Know your microscopic enemy
‚ùì

What Are Dust Mites?

Microscopic arachnids (relatives of spiders) invisible to naked eye. They feed on dead skin cells humans shed daily. A single mattress can harbor millions of dust mites.

‚ö†Ô∏è

Health Impact

Mite feces and decomposing bodies contain proteins triggering 80% of allergies worldwide. Primary cause of allergic rhinitis, asthma, eczema, skin rashes, and sleep disturbances.

üè†

Where They Hide

Thrive in warm, humid environments: mattresses (especially), pillows, sofas, carpets, curtains. Most active at night when humidity is higher. Burrow deep during day.

üéØ

Prevention Strategy

Regular vacuuming with UV-C technology, maintain 50% humidity or less, wash bedding at 60¬∞C+ monthly, air mattresses daily, use mattress protectors, reduce bedroom clutter.

üí° Did You Know?

Humans shed approximately 1.5 grams of dead skin cells daily - enough to feed millions of dust mites. A single dust mite produces about 20 waste droppings per day, and these allergen-laden droppings can remain potent for years even after the mite dies.
